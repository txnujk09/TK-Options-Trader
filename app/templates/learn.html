Learn about Options Trading

Introduction to Options Trading

Options trading allows investors to hedge risk, speculate on market movements, and leverage their positions. An option is a financial derivative that gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an asset at a specific price before a certain date.

Types of Options

  • Call Options: The right to buy an asset at a set price.
  • Put Options: The right to sell an asset at a set price.

Key Concepts

  • Strike Price: The price at which the option can be exercised.
  • Expiration Date: The date on which the option contract expires.
  • Premium: The cost of purchasing an option.
  • Implied Volatility: A measure of the market's expectation of future volatility.
